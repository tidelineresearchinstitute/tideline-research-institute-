Forgotten on the Water

Abandoned vessels and the lasting environmental impacts they leave behind.

When Boats Become Pollution

Abandoned vessels are more than forgotten pieces of maritime history. As they deteriorate, they can release fuel residues, oil, fiberglass, plastics, treated wood, and other materials into surrounding waterways. In tidal environments, these vessels may remain for years, gradually breaking apart while affecting navigation, habitat, and water quality.

Environmental Concerns

  • Fuel and oil contamination.
  • Fiberglass and plastic debris entering waterways.
  • Treated wood slowly deteriorating.
  • Hazards to recreational boaters.
  • Damage to marsh habitat and shoreline vegetation.
  • Reduced aesthetic and ecological value.
