The Hidden Cost of Illegal Tire Dumping

How discarded tires threaten our waterways, wildlife, and communities.

Every Tire Tells a Story

A single discarded tire may appear harmless, but illegally dumped tires can remain in the environment for decades. They collect standing water, create mosquito breeding habitat, release pollutants as they weather, and damage the natural beauty of our communities.

Mosquito Breeding

One discarded tire can collect enough rainwater to support mosquito larvae. Because the dark rubber absorbs heat while shielding water from predators, tires create ideal breeding conditions for mosquitoes capable of transmitting diseases such as West Nile virus and Eastern Equine Encephalitis.

Environmental Hazards

  • Standing water encourages mosquito populations.
  • Illegal dumping damages wildlife habitat.
  • Tires contribute to microplastic pollution as they slowly degrade.
  • Tire piles present significant fire hazards.
  • Runoff from tire fires can contaminate nearby waterways.
  • Dump sites reduce recreational and aesthetic value.

How You Can Help

• Report illegal dumping.

• Dispose of tires through approved recycling programs.

• Participate in community cleanup events.

• Educate others about the environmental impacts of illegal dumping.
